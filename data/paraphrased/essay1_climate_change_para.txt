Global warming stands as among the most critical challenges confronting Earth in modern times. Since the late 1800s, planetary temperatures have climbed approximately 1.1 degrees Celsius, primarily because human activities release greater amounts of carbon dioxide. Burning coal, oil, and natural gas for power generation, heating, and vehicles represents the main cause of these releases. Higher temperatures trigger increasingly common and intense natural disasters such as tropical storms, dry spells, and extreme heat. Polar glaciers are dissolving rapidly, causing ocean levels to rise and endanger seaside populations across the globe. Moreover, shifting weather patterns interfere with farming operations, creating hunger risks in susceptible areas. Tackling this environmental crisis demands worldwide cooperation to adopt clean power alternatives, enhance energy conservation, and safeguard natural environments that absorb carbon. Personal choices also contribute — cutting waste, choosing buses and trains, and backing eco-friendly regulations together create substantial impact. The opportunity for effective intervention is closing, requiring urgent and persistent action.
