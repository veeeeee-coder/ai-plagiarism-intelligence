Mental health has historically been stigmatized and overlooked in many societies, but awareness is finally growing. Conditions like depression and anxiety affect millions of people worldwide, yet many suffer in silence due to fear of judgment. The COVID-19 pandemic dramatically increased mental health challenges as isolation, uncertainty, and grief took their toll. Young people appear particularly vulnerable, with rising rates of self-harm and suicidal ideation reported globally. Access to mental health services remains inadequate, especially in low-income countries where there may be fewer than one psychiatrist per 100,000 people. Workplace stress and burnout have become epidemic in many industries, leading to decreased productivity and personal suffering. Social media presents a double-edged sword — connecting people while also promoting unrealistic comparisons and cyberbullying. Effective mental health care requires a combination of professional treatment, social support, and lifestyle interventions. Governments need to invest more in mental health infrastructure and integrate it into primary care. Reducing stigma through education and open conversation is perhaps the most important step.
